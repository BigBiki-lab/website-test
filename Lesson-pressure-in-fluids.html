Pressure in fluids

Learning objective: How is pressure related to force and area?

What is a simple definition of pressure?

The force exerted perpendicular to a surface per nit area. p=F/A

What are the units for pressure?

pascals, Pa

State two factors that affect the pressure from your shoes on the floor.

force/weight, area of shoes

Explain how wearing snowshoes changes the pressure under your feet.

greater area reduces pressure

The snowshoes spread their weight over a large area so the pressure on the snow is less.

Pressure is a measure of the force on a unit of surface area, where the force is normal (at right angles) to the surface.

Pressure, force and area are related by this equation.

Pressure equation - rearranging

Draw a formula triangle for the equation p=F/A

Use your triangle to rearrange the formula to make force (F) the subject.

F=pxA

Use your triangle to rearrange the formula to make area (A) the subject.

A=F/p

Pressure can also be exerted by fluids (liquids and gases).

There is pressure on you all the time from the air around you.

At sea level, this pressure from the air, or atmospheric pressure, is about 100000 Pa.

Pressure caused by fluids always acts normal to any surface.
