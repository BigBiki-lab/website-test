What does reflection mean?

light/a wave bouncing off something

What is the word for light changing direction when it goes from one material into a different material?

refraction

What is the angle of incidence?

angle between normal and incident/incoming light ray

What is a normal?

line at right angles to a surface

Draw and label a ray diagram to show reflection of light from a plane mirror.

When waves are reflected, the angle of reflection is equal to the angle of incidence.

This is called the law of reflection.

Draw and label a ray diagram to show the refraction of light from air into glass.

Light travels at different speeds in different materials.

It travels faster in air than it does in water or glass.

When a ray of light moves into a material where it travels at a different speed, it usually changes direction.

This is called refraction.

When light meets the interface (boundary) at right angles to it (i.e. along the normal) there is no change in direction.
