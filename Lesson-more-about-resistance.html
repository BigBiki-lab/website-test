Fixed resistor, filament lamp and diode

Draw a graph of current against potential difference for a fixed resistor.

Describe the current–potential difference graph for a fixed resistor.

The graph is linear, showing that as the potential difference increases, the current also increases.

Describe the current–potential difference graph for a filament lamp.

The graph is curved and non-linear. As the potential difference increases, the current increases.

Describe the current–potential difference graph for a diode.

The graph is non-linear. In the forward direction, current only starts to flow when the potential difference reaches a certain value, after which the current increases rapidly.

LDR - Light-Dependent Resistor

A light-dependent resistor has a high resistance in the dark but the resistance gets smaller when the light intensity increases.

Uses of an LDR: automatic street lights, night lights, burglar alarms, camera light meters, automatic brightness control for screens and lamps.

Thermistor

Thermistors have high resistances at low temperatures but as the temperature increases the resistance decreases.

Uses of thermistors: thermostats, digital thermometers, fire alarms, temperature sensors in appliances, central heating systems.

LED - Light-Emitting Diode

It only works in one direction, so if the current is reversed, the LED does not light up.

Uses of LEDs: indicator lights on electronic devices (e.g. TVs, chargers), traffic lights, car headlights and brake lights, displays (phones, calculators, digital clocks), decorative and Christmas lights.
