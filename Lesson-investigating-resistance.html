Investigating resistance

Learning objective: Be able to describe and explain the core practical and its results.

State what resistance is and the units it is measured in.

RESISTANCE

A way of saying how difficult it is for electricity to flow through something. Resistance is measured in units called ohms (Ω).

Draw a series circuit with a battery, bulb, variable resistor and switch in it.

State the equation that links together resistance, current and potential difference.

R = V/I

Calculate the resistance in a circuit when the potential difference ia 9 V and the current is 0.3 A.

R = 30 Ω

Describe the trend shown by your graph - Current increases as the potential difference increases. Resistance of resistor stays constant whereas the resistance of the lamp varies.

Explain the trend shown by your graph - Current increases with the potential difference because the resistance stays the same and R=V/I. The resistance of the lamp increases because the current causes the lamp to heat up, increasing the resistance.

Evaluate the practical and explain improvements that could be made - Compare results with other groups, repeat for different components (diodes etc.)

Describe the trend shown by the results - Potential difference is the same everywhere in a parallel circuit but the potential difference across each component in a series circuit adds up to the potential difference across the power pack. Current is the same everywhere in a series circuit, current for each of the lamps in parallel adds up to the current from the power pack.

Explain the trend shown by the results - Current is conserved in series circuits but in parallel the current splits at a junction to travel along different branches. The potential difference across each branch of a parallel circuit is the same but if there are multiple components in a branch the potential difference across each of them adds up to give the total potential difference supplied by the battery, as in a series circuit.
