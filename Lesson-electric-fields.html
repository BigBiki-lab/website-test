Electric fields

Learning objective: How do electric fields help to explain static electrical effects?

Which charged particles move when something gains a charge?

electrons

If you walk across a nylon carpet you may pick up a charge. Why does this charge not leave you?

nylon is an insulator

If you have a negative charge, why might you feel a shock when touching a metal door handle?

electrons move (from you to the metal)

What is the process of removing charge from an object called?

earthing

magnetic, gravitational and electric/electrostatic force fields

The lines are called field lines and they:

- never cross

- show where the field is strongest (which is where the field lines are closest together).

- show the direction of the force on a charge in the field

- start on a positively charged object, for example the point charge (single point), and end on a negative charged object.

If there is only one object they keep going, becoming more widely spaced.
