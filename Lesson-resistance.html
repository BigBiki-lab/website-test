Resistors in series

When resistors are connected in series the total resistance of the circuit is increased because the pathway becomes harder for current to flow through.

The potential difference from a cell is shared between the resistors, but it may not be shared equally.

There will be greater potential difference across resistors with higher resistances.

Resistors in parallel

When resistors are connected in parallel the total resistance of the circuit is less than the resistance of the individual resistors.

This is because there are now more paths for the current.
