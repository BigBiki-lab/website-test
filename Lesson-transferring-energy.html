Learning objective: How can the energy transfer that causes the heating effect be explained?

The component with resistance that increases when the light intensity decreases is called a ___ ___ ___.

light-dependent resistor

The component with resistance that decreases when the temperature increases is called a _____.

thermistor

The component that passes current in one direction but not the other is called a _____.

diode

Draw a circuit diagram for a circuit to measure the resistance of a light-dependent resistor (LDR). It will need a battery, an ammeter, a voltmeter and the LDR.

All circuits have some resistance, so they warm up when there is a current.

When a current passes through a resistor, energy is transferred because electrical work is done against the resistance.

The energy is transferred by heating and the resistor becomes warm.
