Describe what you see using appropriate scientific vocabulary for a GCSE-level response.

The intensity (amount) of radiation emitted by an object increases as its temperature increases.

The wavelengths of the radiation emitted also change with temperature - the higher the temperature the shorter the wavelengths.

Explain which emits more radiation, a cup of tea at 75°C or a bowl of soup at 50°C?

Answer:The tea at 75°C, as hotter objects emit more radiation.

All the lava in photo is hot but only some of it is hot enough to emit radiation in visible wavelengths.

Explain why astronomers think that blue stars are hotter than yellow stars.

Answer:The hotter the object, the shorter the wavelength at which it emits most radiation. Blue light has a shorter wavelength than yellow light.
