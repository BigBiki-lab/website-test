Learning objective: What is the connection between the electric current and the amount of charge that flows in a circuit?

Which component is used to measure electric current?

ammeter

What two conditions are needed to give a current in a circuit?

closed circuit and potential difference

Is an ammeter connected in parallel od in series with a component?

series

Current is described as the rate of flow of charge. In a metal, what are the charged particles that flow?

electrons

Complete the summary table below.

Moving charged particles form an electric current.

Electric charge is measured in coulombs (C).

One coulomb is the charge that passes a point in a circuit when there is a current of 1 amp for 1 second.

Electric current is the rate of flow of charge.

Write the name of the unit for charge and its symbol.

coulomb, C

Imagine that the current in a circuit doubles. What happens to the amount of charge that passes through the circuit each second?

doubles

With the current doubled, what happens to the time needed for the same total amount of charge to flow?

halves

The current in a circuit is switched on for twice as long. What happens to the total charge that flows?

doubles

The current in a lamp is 0.6 A. How much charge flows through it in 1 minute?

Answer: Q = 36 C

There is a current of 0.3 A in a circuit. How long will it take for 36 C to flow past a point in the circuit?

Answer: t = 120 s
