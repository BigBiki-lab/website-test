What particles flow to form an electrical current?

electrons

What name is given to materials that cannot allow electrons to flow through them?

insulators

Describe the force that holds electrons in an atom. (2 marks)

Show Answer
Electrons are held in the atom by the electrostatic force of attraction between the negatively charged electrons and the positively charged nucleus.

Explain how the strength of the attraction between the nucleus and outer electrons affects whether a material is a conductor or an insulator. (4 marks)

Show Answer
If the attraction between the nucleus and the outer electrons is weak, the electrons can move freely, making the material a conductor. If the attraction is strong, the electrons remain bound to the atoms and cannot move freely, making the material an insulator.

Explain how the nucleus stays stable even though it contains positively charged protons. (3–4 marks)

Show Answer
The nucleus stays stable because the strong nuclear force acts between protons and neutrons and is very strong over short distances. It overcomes the repulsive electrostatic force between the positively charged protons, holding the nucleus together.
