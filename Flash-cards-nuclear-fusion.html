Nuclear fusion

What happens during nuclear fusion?

Two light nuclei combine to form a heavier nucleus.

Name the two isotopes of hydrogen commonly used in nuclear fusion.

Deuterium and Tritium

Where does nuclear fusion naturally occur?

in stars

What conditions are required for nuclear fusion to occur?

Extremely high temperature and high pressure.

Why are extremely high temperatures needed for nuclear fusion to occur?

Give nuclei enough kinetic energy. Overcome the electrostatic repulsion between positively charged nuclei.

What force must be overcome for fusion to happen?

Must overcome electrostatic repulsion between protons.

What force holds the nucleus together?

Strong nuclear force then holds the nucleus together.

State two differences between nuclear fusion and nuclear fission.

Fusion combines nuclei, uses light elements, produces more energy per reaction; fission splits nuclei, uses heavy elements.
