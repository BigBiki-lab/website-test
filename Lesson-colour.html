The light from the Sun or from lamps is called white light.

White light is actually a mixture of different colours of light that our eyes see as white.

White light can be split up into the colours of the visible spectrum using a prism.

When white light hits a coloured surface, some of the colours that make it up are absorbed and some are reflected.

A yellow object looks yellow because it reflects yellow light and absorbs all the other colours.

A white object looks white because it reflects all of the colours.

Filters

Filters are pieces of transparent material that absorb some of the colours in white light.

For example a blue filter transmits (allows through) blue light and absorbs all the other colours.
