Describe how energy is transferred in a battery operated fan.

A some chemical energy stored in the battery is transferred by electricity to the motor, where it is transferred to a store of kinetic energy in the fan.

Some energy will also be transferred by heating the wires, the motor and the surroundings.

In the end, all the energy will be dissipated by heating, making the surroundings a little warmer (increasing their store of thermal energy).

Describe how energy is transferred in a battery-operated gloves.

The gloves contain wire that has a high resistance.

Energy stored in the battery is transferred by electricity to the high resistance wire where it is transferred by heating to a store of thermal energy in the wire.

The energy is then transferred by heating to the gloves and hands of the wearer, and eventually dissipates to the surroundings.

Mains electricity

Many appliances use mains electricity because it can provide the power they need to operate.

In a power station energy is transferred from a store of kinetic energy (such as a turbine) by electricity.

The electricity is carried to our homes through a network of wires and cables known as the national grid.

Compare AC and DC in terms of the movement of charges.

Answer:

In direct current (DC) the charges move in one direction but in alternative current (AC) the charges move back and forth.
