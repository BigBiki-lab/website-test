Nuclear fission

What is nuclear fission?

The splitting of a large/heavy nucleus into two smaller nuclei.

Name a fuel commonly used in nuclear fission reactors.

Uranium-235 or Plutonium-239

What particle is absorbed by a nucleus to start fission?

a neutron

What is released when a nucleus undergoes fission? (give two things)

energy, neutrons, smaller nuclei (daughter nuclei)

What is a chain reaction?

A self-sustaining series of reactions where neutrons from one fission cause further fission.

Why must a chain reaction be controlled in a nuclear reactor?

To prevent the reaction from becoming too fast/uncontrolled, which could cause an explosion.

What is the role of control rods in a nuclear reactor?

Control rods absorb neutrons to control the rate of the reaction.

What is the role of a moderator in a nuclear reactor?

To slow down fast neutrons so they are more likely to cause further fission.
