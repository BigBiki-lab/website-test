Sodium is a metal. There is one electron in the outer shell that is only weakly attracted to the nucleus. All metals have electrons like this, including copper which is used for electrical wiring.

These electrons can easily be removed, so a metal wire has many 'free' electrons.

When a battery is attached to the wire, the voltage 'pushes' the free electrons around the circuit.

The electrons are negatively charged so they move towards the positive terminal of the battery.

Components in circuits can be connected in series or parallel.

In series circuits there is just one route the current can take around the circuit.

In parallel circuits there are junctions that allow the current to take different routes.
