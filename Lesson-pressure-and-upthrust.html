Pressure and upthrust

Learning objective:H Explain and calculate how pressure depends on both depth and density in a fluid.

How does pressure in a fluid change with depth?

Increases

Pressure in a fluid exerts a force. In which direction does the force act?

normal to a surface

Why is there a greater pressure beneath 10m of water than beneath 10m of air?

water is much denser than air

What is the force called that acts upwards on objects in water?

upthrust

The pressure at any point in a fluid depends on the weight of the fluid above.

As an animal descends deeper into the sea, there is more water above it and so the pressure on it increases.

In a fluid with a greater density, the same volume of fluid above will have a greater weight, and so the pressure will be greater.

Pressure

Pressure in the atmosphere ___________ with height.

As the density of the atmosphere increases, the atmospheric pressure ___________.
